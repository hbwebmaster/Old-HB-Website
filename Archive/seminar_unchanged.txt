    <h1>Honor Board <strong>Ethics Course</strong></h1>
<b><font SIZE="5">
<p>I</font><font SIZE="4">NTRODUCTION </p>
</font></b><font FACE="Times New Roman,Times New Roman" SIZE="3">
<p>This Ethics Course is designed for students convicted of an Honor System 
Violation. It is a learning experience which will provide the students with a 
chance for reflection and growth. The course consists of mandatory and elective 
tasks, and an Honor Board representative will be assigned to help guide the 
student through the process. </p>
</font><b><font SIZE="5">
<p>O</font><font SIZE="4">BJECTIVES</font></p>
</b>
<ul>
	<li><font FACE="Times New Roman,Times New Roman" SIZE="3">The students will 
	choose their own plan for self-improvement and reflect on their past 
	actions. </li>
	<li>&nbsp;The students will gain a deeper understanding of the concept of 
	ethics and how ethical behavior should and can be incorporated into both 
	professional and academic life. </li>
	<li>&nbsp;The students will gain a deeper understanding and respect for the 
	Stevens Honor System and learn how to support and uphold it. </li>
</ul>
</font><b><font SIZE="5">
<p>R</font><font SIZE="4">EQUIREMENTS </p>
</font></b><font FACE="Times New Roman,Times New Roman" SIZE="3">
<p>Before beginning the course, the student must contact the Honor Board and 
announce his intent to fulfill the course requirements. An Honor Board member 
will be assigned to serve as the student&#8217;s Mentor and advocate. The student must 
meet with the Mentor to create a proposal detailing his plans for completion of 
the course; the proposal will include the elective components the student has 
chosen. The Mentor will serve as an Advisor, answer the student&#8217;s questions, and 
clarify any uncertainties. </p>
<p>The Ethics Course consists of three components which are described in detail 
in the following sections. The components are:</p>
<p>1. An Ethics Journal kept throughout the progression of the course </p>
<p>2. A Reflection Paper following the completion of all other items in the 
course </p>
<p>3. Elective Tasks ** </p>
<p>**Each Elective Task is weighted in accordance with its difficulty and 
opportunities for growth. The student&#8217;s proposal must include at least 12 points 
worth of elective tasks. The student must choose from the following list of 
elective tasks, but has the option to generate a self-initiated task subject to 
approval by the Honor Board Mentor. </p>
</font>

<p>M</font><font SIZE="3">ANDATORY </font><font SIZE="4">C</font><font SIZE="3">OMPONENTS
</p>
<p>P</font><font SIZE="2">ROPOSAL </p>
</font></b><font FACE="Times New Roman,Times New Roman" SIZE="3">
<p>The student must complete his proposal before beginning the course. The 
proposal must include three sections: intent, goals, and plan. </p>
</font><font SIZE="3"><b>
<p>Intent: </b></font><font FACE="Times New Roman,Times New Roman" SIZE="3">Why 
the student is completing the course. </p>
</font><font SIZE="3"><b>
<p>Goals: </b></font><font FACE="Times New Roman,Times New Roman" SIZE="3">What 
the student hopes to gain by completing the course, including his personal areas 
of improvement. </p>
</font><font SIZE="3"><b>
<p>Plan: </b></font><font FACE="Times New Roman,Times New Roman" SIZE="3">The 
specific tasks that the student intends to complete and his reasons for choosing 
those tasks. </p>
<p>After the student has completed writing his proposal, he must sign it and 
give it to his Honor Board Mentor. The proposal is subject to approval by the 
Honor Board Mentor and Advisor. </p>
<p>If the Honor Board Advisor approves the proposal, the student will be 
notified and may begin completing his tasks. If the Honor Board Advisor does not 
approve the proposal, the student will have the opportunity to revise and 
resubmit it. </p>
</font><b><font SIZE="3">
<p>E</font><font SIZE="2">THICS </font><font SIZE="3">J</font><font SIZE="2">OURNAL
</p>
</font></b><font FACE="Times New Roman,Times New Roman" SIZE="3">
<p>Throughout the Ethics Course, the student will be required to keep an Ethics 
Journal. The purpose of the Ethics Journal is to track the student&#8217;s progress in 
the course, provide an opportunity to reflect on each of the completed tasks, 
and provide an overall reflection of the learning experience. It is recommended 
that the student write in it at least once per week throughout the course. The 
journal may be in any format, but must include the following. 6 </p>
<p>There must be an entry in this journal corresponding to each </font>
<font SIZE="3"><b>elective </b></font>
<font FACE="Times New Roman,Times New Roman" SIZE="3">task completed during the 
course. The entry must address the following questions: </p>
<p>1. What task was completed? What was done to complete it? </p>
<p>2. What was learned from this task? Was it a good learning experience? How 
did it help the student grow as a person? </p>
<p>3. What is the student&#8217;s feedback about this task? (The student may choose to 
write negative things about the tasks in this journal. This is the Honor Board&#8217;s 
chance to get criticism or advice from the student about the course!) </p>
<p>The student may include additional entries of his choosing. A suggestion for 
further reflection is to comment on an ethical dilemma or difficult decision 
faced during this period. Some questions that may be addressed are: </p>
<p>1. What was the situation? Was it a hard decision to make? </p>
<p>2. What were the pros and cons of each option? </p>
<p>3. What option was chosen and why? </p>
<p>4. What was the impact of this decision? </p>
</font><b><font SIZE="3">
<p>R</font><font SIZE="2">EFLECTION </font><font SIZE="3">P</font><font SIZE="2">APER
</p>
</font></b><font FACE="Times New Roman,Times New Roman" SIZE="3">
<p>The Reflection Paper is the final requirement of the course. It is not to be 
written until all of the other tasks have been completed. It should be very
</font><font SIZE="3"><b>personal </b></font>
<font FACE="Times New Roman,Times New Roman" SIZE="3">and should talk about the 
student&#8217;s experience with the Ethics Course and how it has helped him grow as a 
person. The Reflection Paper must be 3-5 pages long and should address: </p>
<p>1. The factors that led to having to complete the Ethics Course </p>
<p>2. Each task that was completed and how it contributed to the student&#8217;s 
learning process </p>
<p>3. What was learned and gained from taking the course and whether the 
personal goals set in the proposal were met </p>
<p>4. How the student plans to implement what he has learned into the rest of 
his life at Stevens and beyond </p>
<p>5. If the student has answered all of the above and still has more to talk 
about, additional thoughts can be recorded in the journal </p>
</font><b><font SIZE="4">
<p>E</font><font SIZE="3">LECTIVE </font><font SIZE="4">C</font><font SIZE="3">OMPONENTS
</font><font SIZE="4">(M</font><font SIZE="3">UST </font><font SIZE="4">C</font><font SIZE="3">OMPLETE 
AT LEAST </font><font SIZE="4">12 </font><font SIZE="3">POINTS</font><font SIZE="4">)
</p>
</font><font SIZE="3">
<p>F</font><font SIZE="2">ORMAL </font><font SIZE="3">A</font><font SIZE="2">POLOGY 
TO THE </font><font SIZE="3">P</font><font SIZE="2">ROFESSOR </p>
</font></b><font FACE="Times New Roman,Times New Roman" SIZE="3">
<p>This is a formal letter which must be written or typed using proper English 
and mailed or given (NOT emailed) to the professor. The Honor Board Mentor must 
approve the contents of the letter before it is sent or given to the professor. 
The letter should reflect a contrite and honest attitude towards the violation 
that was committed. This task is worth 2 points towards completion of the 
course. </p>
</font><b><font SIZE="3">
<p>F</font><font SIZE="2">ORMAL </font><font SIZE="3">A</font><font SIZE="2">POLOGY 
TO </font><font SIZE="3">S</font><font SIZE="2">TUDENTS </p>
</font></b><font FACE="Times New Roman,Times New Roman" SIZE="3">
<p>If the violation involved treating another student or students without 
respect by &quot;cheating&quot; or &quot;copying&quot; off of them, or any other similar action, the 
student may choose to write a formal letter of apology. The letter must be 
written or typed using proper English and mailed (NOT emailed) or given directly 
to the student or students who were disrespected. If the violation did not hurt 
a specific student, a letter of apology may be sent to the entire class. If 
sending the letter to the entire class, it may be read aloud to the class. The 
letter should be at least two paragraphs in length and reflect a contrite and 
honest attitude towards the violation that was committed. The Honor Board Mentor 
must approve the contents of the letter before it is sent or given to the 
student(s) or the class. This task is worth 2 points towards completion of the 
course. 8 </p>
<b>
<p>C</b></font><b><font FACE="Times New Roman,Times New Roman" SIZE="2">OMMUNITY
</font><font FACE="Times New Roman,Times New Roman" SIZE="3">S</font><font FACE="Times New Roman,Times New Roman" SIZE="2">ERVICE
</p>
</font></b><font FACE="Times New Roman,Times New Roman" SIZE="3">
<p>The student may elect to do up to 8 hours of community service as a part of 
the Ethics Course. Each hour that is completed is worth ¼ point towards 
completion of the course. The organization that the student chooses to work with 
must be indicated in his proposal along with an explanation as to how doing this 
community service will contribute to the learning and recovery process. The 
student should not be receiving credit for this community service for any other 
club, team, or organization. The supervisor&#8217;s name and contact phone number must 
be indicated in the proposal. When the student has finished his community 
service hours, both he and the Honor Board Mentor will sign this sheet. </p>
</font><b><font SIZE="3">
<p>R</font><font SIZE="2">EDO THE </font><font SIZE="3">A</font><font SIZE="2">SSIGNMENT
</p>
</font></b><font FACE="Times New Roman,Times New Roman" SIZE="3">
<p>The student may retake the assignment on which his Honor System violation 
occurred. Retaking the assignment will NOT count for credit in the class. The 
student is expected to put his full effort into this assignment, as if it were 
to be graded. After completing the assignment, it is highly recommended that the 
student submit it to the professor as a way to show that he is serious about 
learning the material with integrity and honor. If the assignment is a quiz or 
homework assignment, the student will earn 1 point; if it is a test, exam, or 
paper, he will earn 2 points. </p>
</font><b><font SIZE="3">
<p>A</font><font SIZE="2">CADEMIC </font><font SIZE="3">S</font><font SIZE="2">EMINARS
</p>
</font></b><font FACE="Times New Roman,Times New Roman" SIZE="3">
<p>The student may choose to attend up to two seminars offered by the Academic 
Support Center. A list of the workshops being offered by the Academic Support 
Center can be found at www.stevens.edu/asc. Some of these workshops may require 
pre-registration, so the student must pay attention to all the details when 
viewing the available options. There may be other seminars offered throughout 
the campus, but not through the Academic Support Center. These seminars also may 
be acceptable if they relate to the personal 9 </p>
<p>improvement goals outlined in the student&#8217;s proposal, and if they are 
approved by his Honor Board Mentor. </p>
<p>When the student attends the seminar must have the presenter of the seminar 
sign to confirm his attendance. Each seminar that the student attends will be 
worth 1 point towards completion of the Ethics Course. </p>
</font><b><font SIZE="3">
<p>&quot;D</font><font SIZE="2">EATH OF </font><font SIZE="3">I</font><font SIZE="2">VAN
</font><font SIZE="3">I</font><font SIZE="2">LYICH</font><font SIZE="3">&quot; (copy 
on reserve in the Samuel C. Williams Library) </p>
</font></b><font FACE="Times New Roman,Times New Roman" SIZE="3">
<p>For this task, the student must read the short novel by Leo Tolstoy and 
respond to the following questions</font><font FACE="Times New Roman,Times New Roman" SIZE="1">1</font><font FACE="Times New Roman,Times New Roman" SIZE="3">. 
There is no length requirement for the responses, although it is important that 
they be of a high quality. This is worth 2 points towards the total value of 
elective tasks. </p>
</font><font FACE="Times New Roman,Times New Roman" SIZE="1">
<p>1 These questions were taken from a course designed by </font>
<font FACE="Times New Roman,Times New Roman" SIZE="2" COLOR="#545454">the 
University of Maryland, Office of Judicial Programs &amp; Student Ethical 
Development. They are being used by the Stevens Honor Board with the permission 
of Gary Pavela, Esq., Director. </p>
</font><font FACE="Times New Roman,Times New Roman" SIZE="3">
<ul class="style4">
	<li>Why did Ivan conclude his life &quot;was not the real thing?&quot; (Consider his 
	interests, values, aims, and his relationships with his family.) </li>
	<li>&nbsp;Why did Ivan stop screaming? (Provide a specific quotation.) </li>
	<li>&nbsp;What does Tolstoy have to say about the attractions of 
	materialism? (Give an example from Ivan&#8217;s life.) </li>
</ul>
</font><b><font SIZE="3">
<p>P</font><font SIZE="2">RESENTATION ON THE </font><font SIZE="3">H</font><font SIZE="2">ONOR
</font><font SIZE="3">S</font><font SIZE="2">YSTEM </p>
</font></b><font FACE="Times New Roman,Times New Roman" SIZE="3">
<p>For 3 points towards completion of the course, the student can choose to give 
a speech on the Honor System to a group of his peers. It must be delivered to at 
least 10 other students who may be members of a club, sports team, Greek house, 
class, or any other group selected by the student. The speech must be at least 
15 minutes long and may utilize any 10 </p>
<p>appropriate audio/ visual aid. The student must state in the proposal who 
will be the audience for the speech and why he chose that group of students. The 
student may choose to use some of the following talking points in his speech or 
come up with his own: </p>
<ul class="style4">
	<li>A history of the Honor System at Stevens </li>
	<li>&nbsp;Duties of students and faculty as outlined in the Honor Board&#8217;s 
	Constitution </li>
	<li>&nbsp;The importance of having an Honor System </li>
	<li>&nbsp;The student&#8217;s violation and his learning experiences as a result 
	of the Ethics Course </li>
</ul>
<p>A draft of the student&#8217;s presentation must be approved by the Honor Board 
Mentor prior to its delivery. Also, the student must alert the Honor Board 
Mentor of the time and place for the speech. It is required that at least one 
Honor Board Representative be present when the speech is made. If no Honor Board 
members are able to attend, the audience members must complete the official 
signature form at the conclusion of the presentation. </p>

<p>E</font><font FACE="Times New Roman,Times New Roman" SIZE="2">SSAYS </p>
</font></b><font SIZE="3">
<p>A list of prompts is available in Appendix F of the ethics course manual, 
which can be downloaded above. The student may choose to write an essay response 
to one or more of these prompts. The response must be 3 to 5 pages in length 
unless noted otherwise and must be well-written with proper English grammar and 
citations, if outside resources are used. Each essay that the student writes 
will be worth 1 point, and he may submit a maximum of three responses. 12 </p>

his Ethics Course. The student must discuss these plans with his Honor Board 
Mentor and be able to justify how the task will contribute to the learning 
process. The task must be indicated in the proposal submitted by the student and 
subsequently be approved by both the Honor Board Mentor and Advisor. When the 
student proposes a self-initiated task, additional guidelines will be determined 
by the student, Honor Board Mentor, and Honor Board Advisor. </p>

<p>C</font><font FACE="Times New Roman,Times New Roman" SIZE="4">OMPLETION OF 
THE </font><font FACE="Times New Roman,Times New Roman" SIZE="5">E</font><font FACE="Times New Roman,Times New Roman" SIZE="4">THICS

</p>

<p>It is the responsibility of both the student and the Honor Board Mentor to 
stay in regular contact with each other to ensure the student&#8217;s smooth 
progression in the course. Following the completion of each task, the student is 
required to submit all required materials to his Honor Board Mentor. The Honor 
Board Mentor shall be responsible for maintaining a folder of all material 
submitted. Once the student has completed and submitted paperwork for all 
mandatory and elective tasks in his proposal, he will sit down with his Honor 
Board Mentor for a final exit interview, at which time the Final Evaluation Form 
must be completed. During the final exit interview, the student and Mentor will 
discuss the student&#8217;s accomplishments and evaluate his overall learning 
experience. </p>
<p>The Final Evaluation Form and all other materials will be submitted to the 
Honor Board Advisor for her approval. The Honor Board Advisor will determine if 
the student has satisfactorily completed the Ethics Course. After reviewing the 
material the Honor Board Advisor may choose to meet with the student, or ask the 
student to redo or revise certain components of the course. When the Honor Board 
Advisor approves the student&#8217;s work and signs the Final Evaluation Form, the 
student is considered to have completed the Ethics Course. </p>
